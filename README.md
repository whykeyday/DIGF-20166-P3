Project Description ：

Blooming Boundaries is an expressive wearable   project inspired by the form of the traditional Chinese cloud collar and blooming flowers. It reinterprets these forms through movement, light, and sound. It uses micro servo motors, pressure sensors, a light sensor, and the CPX to explore how the body shifts between openness and self protection during social interaction through movement, sound, and light.
This wearable is built onto a white jacket. Four servos are placed at the front and back of both shoulders. Based on the servo arm parts provided by the instructor, I redesigned and made my own 3D printed bases and arm structures with sewing holes. I then connected thin metal wire and tulle to the servo arms to form flower like shapes that can rise, open, and close around the neck and face. The final wearable appears delicate and beautiful, but it can quickly transform into a protective structure. 
The project responds to three different situations. The first behavior is triggered by pressure near the rib area. When the wearer feels uncomfortable, embarrassed, angry, tired, or emotionally tense, they may tightly cross their arms over their chest. At this moment, the wearable flower rises upward and forms a collar like shield that covers the face and creates a protected personal space. The second situation is triggered by a pressure sensor on the hand during a handshake or intentional touch with another person. In this case, the flowers don't move. Instead, the CPX produces a sound that is not harsh or alarming, while rainbow running lights appear at the same time. This expresses friendliness, but also gently reminds others that personal boundaries still exist. The third response is triggered by strong light. Under bright light, the flowers repeatedly open and close in a breathing rhythm, suggesting the tension of being watched, exposed, or placed under attention.
This project explores how wearable technology can make invisible psychological boundaries visible. It shows how people constantly move between openness and defense under social attention, bodily pressure, and interpersonal contact. It doesn't treat self protection as a sign of weakness. Instead, it understands self protection as a meaningful bodily response, a form of body wisdom, a way of expressing autonomy, sensitivity, and emotional self regulation, and an active expression of personal boundaries.
